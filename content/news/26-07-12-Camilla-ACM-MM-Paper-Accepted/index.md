Camilla's full paper, **"Beyond Modality Harmony: Orthogonal Purification and Topology-Guided MoE for Conflict-Aware Multimodal Recommendation,"** has been accepted to the **ACM Multimedia 2026 Main Track**!

<!--more-->

Congratulations to **Camilla** on this outstanding achievement. ACM Multimedia (ACM MM) is a flagship conference in the multimedia field, and this year's main track received 7,053 submissions with an acceptance rate of around 20%. The 34th ACM MM will take place in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, from November 10 to 14, 2026.

This work addresses conflict-aware multimodal recommendation through orthogonal purification and a topology-guided mixture-of-experts approach, making a valuable contribution to multimodal AI research.

Camilla shares her sincere thanks to **Prof. Ray**, **Prof. Jenny**, and all CALAS members for their support, guidance, and collaboration. She looks forward to producing more high-quality research and working more closely with the team in the future. She also thanks everyone for the excellent teamwork during today's TBL.

Congratulations again to Camilla on this well-deserved acceptance!
