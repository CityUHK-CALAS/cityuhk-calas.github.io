It brings us great pleasure to share that PhD students Simon and Henry have achieved a remarkable milestone with their paper acceptance at ISFPGA 2026!
<!--more-->

<div style="text-align: justify">

![](image1.png)
Their work, titled "ViM-Q: Energy-Efficient Algorithm-Hardware Co-Design for Dynamically Quantized Vision Mamba Models," will be presented at the ACM/SIGDA International Symposium on Field-Programmable Gate Arrays in Monterey, California, from February 22-24, 2026.

ISFPGA stands as the premier international conference for advances in FPGA technology, bringing together leading researchers and industry experts from around the world. This 34th edition provides an exceptional platform for showcasing cutting-edge research in algorithm-hardware co-design and AI/ML acceleration.

Simon and Henry's work on energy-efficient Vision Mamba implementations demonstrates CALAS's continued excellence in advancing the frontier of FPGA-based AI acceleration. Congratulations on this outstanding achievement!
